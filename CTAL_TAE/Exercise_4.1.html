Exercise 4.1: Pilot & deployment guidelines.

Learning Objective ALTA-E 4.1.1 (K3) Apply guidelines that support effective test tool pilot and deployment activities.
Time 40 Minutes
Version 1.1

Re-read the System Description phase 1.

Your company has just been bought by GoodCorp Inc. They were mainly interested in the music you produce and sell. After the sale, they noticed your web application. Since their web applications are quite old and not up to current web standards, they are interested in using them for the different kind of goods they are selling. Of course, your Test Automation Architecture would also be used.

The different groups in GoodCorp Inc. are:

  • Group A: They think they will be able to use your application without change. They also use Jira, but have a different defect lifecycle defined. Their current application is written in Java with Eclipse as IDE and SVN as source control system. Their team consists of 2 senior Java developers, 1 junior and 1 tester with 5 years' experience who can write basic test cases in Java.
  • Group B: Group B sells home automation systems but does this in the form of a group sale. People can register to buy goods like actuators and switches. Group B then searches vendors that can sell and install the goods at a lower price due to the volume. They foresee major modifications to your applications, both in the front- and back-end. The Front-end will need to be modified to support the group sale model. They intend to use the backend to communicate with their suppliers to send daily information on who has bought the items. Since this group was just formed, they don't have developers in their team yet. They expect your team to make the changes. They do intend to hire 1 experienced tester that will focus on their version of the applications. They haven't decided yet on a defect tracker.
  • Group C: The manager of Group C has briefly assessed your application. They think a new micro-service-based application will need to be written to replace the frontend & backend. They use a different commercial defect tracker and haven't decided yet on the development environment.
